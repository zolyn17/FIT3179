Suicide Rates Overview 1985 to 2016

To visualise the suicide rates over the years in different countries as well as the relationship between the number of suicide cases and other factors such as gender, economy, age group and their respective generations.

Suicides Cases By Country and GDP

We can see that the United States and the Russian Federation has the highest number of suicide cases. An interesting finding is that although Africa has a lower GDP Per Capita their rate of suicide is 0.08 which is lower compared to the US that is 1.28. The Russian Federation which belongs to Europe has a very high rate of suicide which is 3.28.

Suicide Cases by Generation

We notice that the number of suicide cases for Generation X has peaked around the year 2014. The suicide cases have been increasing substantially for the Millenials but it has decreased after 2014. As for generation Boomers, the suicide cases are the highest during the year 1996. Besides that, the number of suicide cases has started decreasing after the year 1990 for generation Silent.

Number of suicide cases by gender and their age group

Male contribute to a higher number of suicide cases as compared to females. In total, the number of suicide cases for Male is 5188910 as compared to females which is only 1559510 . We also realise that the age-group that have the highest number of suicide cases for both of the gender is 34-54 years, followed by 55-74 years.
